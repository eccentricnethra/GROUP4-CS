What is Optical Fiber?

Optical fibers are very thin wires which transmit data with light pulses. The fibers in the Optical fiber are usually made using Silica-based glass or Plastic, they are also insulators and they do not have any currents flowing in them. These fibers are often used in the transmission of high bandwidth data at high speed. Fibers are highly thin, sometimes even as thin as a human hair strand. With the growth of technology and dependency on the internet, optical fiber technology has become one of the most important and majorly used technology these days. These Optical Fibers work by a mechanism called total internal reflection, this is when the light which passes through the cable refracts through the fiber.

 

 

Advantages of optical fibers:

 

    •Thin and non-flammable
    •Economical and cost-effective
    •Less power consumption
    •Excellent data privacy
    •Less signal degradation

 

Disadvantages of optical fibers:

 

    •High cost
    High light power emitting sources are expensive

 

Data loss also occurs in Optical fibers just like any other cable, While Optical Fibers are very much superior, in terms of data transfer, then any other medium, it still comes with its own problems. One of which being data loss while transferring data. This can happen due to many reasons.

 

    • First way that data loss while transferring data can happen is by Intrinsic Fiber Core Attenuation. This refers to problems in the inside of the optic fiber. The first way this can happen is through light absorption. This is when the materials of the optic fiber absorb the light that is carrying the data. This is then converted to heat because of molecular resonance as well as wavelength impurities. Another example of Intrinsic Fiber Core Attenuation is scattering. This refers to light not moving in a straight path but rather in a very random motion. Some of the light continues to travel in a straight path but the others will just forever be bouncing by hitting the internal surface of the fiber.

 

    • The second way data loss while transferring data can happen is Extrinsic Fiber Attenuation. This refers to external problems that can affect. This also comes under 2 ways. First one being bend loss. This refers to the mishandling of the optical fiber causing it to bend. Micro bending refers to a small bend while macro bending refers to a large-scale bending. The second way of Extrinsic Fiber Attenuation happens with Fiber Optic Splicing. This refers to the joining of 2 optical fibers with the use of heat.

 

Some other possible reasons for data loss in optical Fibers, overheating, Water or Fire damage, Exposure to magnetic fields, Power outages or surges not completely connected to cables, collisions of data through hub, and corruption.

 

Data can also be lost from other important factors.When the Light signal reaches the HUB, as usual the HUB splits this light to the other ports, Optical splitters, also known as beam splitters, are used in this process to split a single light beam into multiple streams. This can cause small loss in strength of the data known as insertion loss. After this the split light is sent to different ports through optical fibers again. Insertion loss is typically measured in decibels. Insertion loss in optical splitters is due to various factors, also considering coupling loss between the splitter and the incoming fiber, the splitting ratio, and the quality of the splitter.

We will be comparing the lengths of 1km, 100km and 10,000km, this is because we can view chromatic dispersion more clearly when there is more length difference, if the difference was something like “ 1km, 2km, 3km “ the difference would be minute.

 

Chromatic dispersion would be the focus of this experiment since it is a factor in data loss during transfer. It is the process of light splitting into different colors like in the prism experiment conducted by Sir Issac Newton. Due to this, different frequencies of light will arrive at different times as each color represents a different wavelength. The reason that this happens is because the optic fiber is made out of a core as well as cladding which comprises different materials each of different refractive index. This becomes an issue since it essentially does an equivalent of reducing the clarity of the defined binary pulses that are being sent through the optic fiber.

Analysis and Evaluation

The first variable that we have taken for this experiment is the wavelength of the light passing through. The change in chromatic dispersion seems to be negligible as it differs from wavelength to wavelength by mere decimals. Our prediction of why this would be the case is because during dispersion of light, it changes from just white light to different colors of light which all have their own wavelength. Due to this, the wavelength of the initial light that passes through would not play a role in the data transfer loss in chromatic dispersion.

 

The length of the optical fiber does play a huge role in chromatic dispersion but not in the way we had anticipated. We had predicted that the longer the length would be, the greater the chromatic dispersion, due to the lack of space there is for chromatic dispersion to occur, hence the greater the data transfer loss would be but it actually reversed. The longer the length of the optic fiber is, the less chromatic dispersion occurs. This is because as the length of the fiber increases the number of propagation modes decreases, in fiber with many propagation modes like the smaller ones, each mode will travel in different velocities, due to chromatic dispersion, causing signal degradation, propagation modes are also called guided modes, they are different ways light can travel through a Fiber, when light is introduced to the fiber it can travel in many ways, and guided/propagation modes can guide them where to go.
